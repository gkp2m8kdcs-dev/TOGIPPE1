Getting started

What does TOGIPPE actually do?

It's two tools in one. The Converter performs bidirectional conversions across unit families (length, mass, energy, pressure, etc.). The Hunter parses any unit expression you type — e.g. kg·m·s⁻² — and resolves it to an SI base dimension vector (M¹·L¹·T⁻², which is force).

Expressions

What syntax does the expression parser accept?

You can combine SI symbols (kg, m, s, A, K, mol, cd) and recognised derived units (N, J, Pa, W, V, Ω, F, H, T, Hz). Use · for multiplication, / for division, and superscripts (², ⁻¹) or ^ for exponents.

Privacy

Does the app send my data anywhere?

No. TOGIPPE runs entirely on-device. There is no account system, no analytics SDK, and no network requests for content. Your recent hunts and preferences live in the iOS app sandbox. See the Privacy Policy for the full breakdown.

Data

How do I clear my recent hunts?

Open Settings → Data → Clear Recent Hunts and confirm. To remove everything, including preferences, simply uninstall the app.

Data

Can I stop the app from remembering anything?

Yes. Toggle off Persist Recent Hunts in Settings. The app will then start with an empty history every launch.

Accuracy

How accurate are the conversions?

Conversion factors follow SI and NIST values. Display precision is configurable from 2 to 8 fractional digits. For safety-critical or regulated calculations, always cross-check against authoritative sources.

Troubleshooting

My expression shows "Unknown" — what happened?

The parser couldn't match every token to a known unit. Check spelling, ensure exponents are attached to the preceding symbol (s⁻², not s -2), and that all symbols are recognised. You can browse the in-app Library for valid units.

Troubleshooting

The app feels slow on first launch.

On a brand-new install the unit catalogue initialises lazily. Subsequent launches are near-instant because the catalogue is cached in memory and preferences are preloaded from UserDefaults.

Devices

Which devices and iOS versions are supported?

TOGIPPE targets modern iPhone and iPad running recent versions of iOS / iPadOS. The app is built for arm64 devices and is portrait-oriented.
